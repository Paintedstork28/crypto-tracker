Fee Breakdown & Gross vs Net Profit

Understanding the Costs

Buy-Side Trading Fee
0.10%
Exchange fee when buying the cheaper stablecoin with your local currency. Charged by WazirX, CoinDCX, Binance etc.
Swap / Convert Fee
0.05%
Fee to swap cheap stablecoin to expensive one. Binance Convert: 0%. DEX (1inch/Uniswap on Polygon): ~0.05%. Ethereum mainnet: higher gas.
Sell-Side Trading Fee
0.10%
Exchange fee when selling the expensive stablecoin back to local currency. Same as buy-side.
Network / Withdrawal Fee
0.02%
Blockchain transfer fee if moving between exchanges. Use Polygon/TRC20 for ~$0.01-$0.10. ERC20 can be $2-5.
Currency Conversion Spread
0.03%
Hidden spread between market exchange rate and the rate you actually get when converting fiat to crypto and back.
Total Estimated Fees
~0.30%
Combined cost per round-trip trade. Your spread must exceed this for profit.
Gross Profit = the raw price difference (spread) between the cheap and expensive stablecoin, multiplied by the number of coins you trade. This ignores all costs.
Gross Profit = (Sell Price - Buy Price) × Number of Coins
Net Profit = what you actually take home after subtracting all fees (trading fees on both sides, swap fee, network fee, and conversion spread).
Net Profit = Gross Profit - Buy Fee - Swap Fee - Sell Fee - Network Fee - Conversion Spread
Example: If spread = 0.50% and total fees = 0.30%, your net profit = 0.20% of the invested amount. On $10,000 invested, that's $20 net profit per round-trip.

Where to Trade

Indian and global platforms that support fiat deposits and stablecoin trading.

Indian exchange with INR pairs for USDT, USDC. Supports P2P and direct bank transfer (UPI/IMPS). Low maker fees (0.1%). Good for INR → USDT entry.

Buy/sell USDT with INR via P2P at competitive rates. Zero maker fees on P2P. Convert between stablecoins on Binance Convert at 0 fees. Best for the swap step.
